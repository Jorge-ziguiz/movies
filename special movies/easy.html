Easy Rider

is a 1969 American independent drama film directed by Dennis Hopper and starring Peter Fonda and Jack Nicholson as lead actors.
It is inspired by the 1962 Italian film The Getaway. Winner of numerous awards and candidate for many others,
the film Easy Rider is considered a milestone of the 1960s counterculture
and a fundamental paradigm in the history of cinema in its country and the world. It is one of the precursors of independent production,
and with it it was raised one hundred times more than its production cost.
Billy (Dennis Hopper) and Wyatt (Peter Fonda) are two young people who embark on a journey crossing the southwestern and southern
United States with the goal of attending the Mardi Gras carnival in New Orleans. To finance their trip, they use cocaine trafficking
on the border with Mexico. With their motorcycles they embark on a journey in which they get to know different faces of American society.
Along the way they meet various and unusual characters, a rancher and his family, a hippie hitchhiker, end up being arrested
in a small town for parading without a permit, where they meet George (Jack Nicholson), an alcoholic lawyer who gets them out of trouble.
jail and then decides to join them.
